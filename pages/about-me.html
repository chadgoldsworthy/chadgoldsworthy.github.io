About Me

Nationality: South African
Location: Cape Town, South Africa
Born: 1995-12-19

I originally studied sound engineering, and worked as a sound and lighting engineer in the live events and theatre industry for a few years. I had always had a passion for technology and data, and would learn programming languages in my spare time. I eventually decided to pursue a career in data engineering, so I decided to study computer science and change my career path.

I graduated with a BSc Honors in Computer Science from Arden University in 2020 (graduating with 1st class), and then started my masters degree in artificial intelligence with the University of Leeds in 2021. I starting working for a data engineering company in 2022, and decided to not finish my masters degree as I found more value in practical experience than in theoretical knowledge. I left with a postgraduate certificate (with distinction).

Outside of work, I am involved with an animal rescue organization, and spend my time fostering animals and helping in their rehabilitation. I also enjoy hiking, and spend my weekends exploring the beautiful mountains and nature reserves in Cape Town with my dogs.

My ultimate goal in life is to use the skills I have learned to work in the field of wildlife conservation, and to use technology to help protect endangered species and their habitats. I believe that technology can play a crucial role in conservation efforts, and I am passionate about using my skills to make a positive impact on the world.
